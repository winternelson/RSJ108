How to Write a Lead

Time Needed: About 30 minutes
Awesome Rating: as awesome as you make it

Having trouble writing a lead for your article? These five easy steps will help you do so in no time!

  • Things You May Need

  • Access to Internet
  • Laptop or Computer
  • Paper
  • Pen/Pencil
  • Time
  • Adequate Information For Your Article

  1. Know your audience.

    One of the most important things to remember when writing a lead is to know your audience. You can write an amazing lead, but if it doesn’t attract the attention of your target audience, it's next to useless. That's why it is best to keep in mind keywords that will attract the attention of the people you want.

  2. Identify the Five Ws and H.

    To write a good lead, it's best to incorporate your Five Ws and H. This means you should include the what of the story, the why, the when, the where, the who, and how. Of course, using all of these might be too much, but it's best to use the main facts to attract people to your story.

    Writing down your main facts is a great way to start with incorporating your five Ws and H.

  3. Make it eye catching.

    In order to draw attention to your lead, make sure it isn't too long. You can catch the eye of your readers by mentioning people they might be interested in, or events that they would be privy to attending.

  4. Remain consistent.

    Your lead is the most important part of the article, but its also best to consider how your lead relates to the next couple paragraphs. You want to make sure the lead flows well and has the same style as the rest of the article.

  5. Edit for clarity.

    Once you’ve written your lead, you're not finished yet! Make sure that your grammar and punctuation is accurate. Names must also be spelled correctly! It’s also best to see how it flows with the rest of your article. Remember, this is the very first thing your reader is going to be seeing. Make sure it's something that hooks them into reading more!
